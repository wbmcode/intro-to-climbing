Climbing Grips

A variety of rock climbing holds exist and they each require different grip types.

Grip Name Description Difficulty
Crimp Perfect for smaller edges, a crimp grip (AKA crimping) is when you can only fit about a single finger pad on a hold. Hard to use
Open-Hand Used for grabbing slopers, the open-hand grip involves creating as much friction as possible on the hold because there is no edge to grab onto. Moderate Difficulty
Pinch Pinching involves grabbing a hold with an open-hand grip or half-crimp, then using the thumb to pinch the other edge of the rock. Moderate difficulty
Pocket A pocket grip uses one or more fingers inside the pocket hole and involved hanging directly beneath the hold as much as possible. Easy to use
